Bharatiya Interface for E-Security & Help Management



    BHISHM, Bhartiya Interface for E-Security and Help Management is a platform where user can file and manage all the complaints regarding any incident concerned to digital transaction and fraud. With the Digital India movement, there has been a rapid growth in the number of digital users. Now people prefer to go cashless than ever before. But there is a lack of credibility in Digital Transaction. There may be few incidents, which create doubts in the mind of users regarding Digital Transaction. BHISHM tries to safeguard the belief of user in Digital India. In case of any incident user can login with his UID and select the victim bank account. Automated complaint will be filled to all the concern department which includes, related branch of the bank, support centre of bank, cyber cell. User can also lodge complaint at his local police station also. All type of user are welcome, whether individual or businessperson. We are here to serve all. All the complaints and acknowledgements will be drafted in form of report. User can use the reports further to reach Ombudsmen or Court. All the reports are digitally signed from related department.

What is Cyber Crime?

A safe and secure online environment enhances trust and confidence and contributes to a stable and productive community. The AFP has a significant role to play in achieving this through its partnerships and proactive and innovative policing. Information and communications technology (ICT) is an integral part of our daily lives. Whether people have a computer at home, use online banking services or simply receive electricity supplies, the community's reliance on technology is increasing. Government and business also take advantage of opportunities for economic development through increased use of information technology and a technology aware population with internet connections locally and overseas. The AFP sees the increasing use and dependence on technology as one of the major influences on the domestic and international law enforcement operating environment. ICT impacts on law enforcement because of the way in which it can facilitate both lawful and unlawful activities. Crimes such as fraud, scams, and harassment can be facilitated by using technology which brings unique challenges to old crimes. Activities which fall under this category are often referred to as high tech crime, computer crimes or cybercrimes. Technology-enabled crime encompasses: Crimes committed directly against computers and computer systems. The use of technology to commit or facilitate the commission of traditional crimes.

Do & Dont's

    Do's

  • Learn how to spot common scams and fraud - Find out the warning signs of internet fraud, phishing, and other online scams.
  • Update your computer software - Download the latest versions of your operating system, web browsers, and apps.
  • Talk to your kids about being safe and responsible online - If you are a parent, help protect your kids online by teaching them about the risks.

    Dont's

  • Don’t share your passwords or sensitive information with anyone you don’t trust.
  • Don’t use the same passwords for multiple accounts - Try to make your passwords unpredictable and avoid using names, dates, or common words.
  • Don’t give out personal information over unencrypted websites - When shopping or banking online, only use websites that use encryption to protect your information.
